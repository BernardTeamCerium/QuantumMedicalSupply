Beyond the Prescription: Improving Access to Compression Therapy for Lymphedema

Presented in collaboration with the Florida Podiatric Medical Association

Video discussion · Featuring Dr. Liana Seldin, DPM, FPMA President-Elect, and Erica Sed of Quantum Medical Supply · 2026

For people living with lymphedema and chronic venous disease, getting the right compression therapy is only half the battle — the harder part is often navigating the barriers that stand between a prescription and consistent, ongoing care. This discussion looks beyond the prescription at how patients, providers, and industry partners can work together to make medically necessary treatment more accessible.

What Erica covers

In this educational discussion presented in collaboration with the Florida Podiatric Medical Association (FPMA), Dr. Liana Seldin, DPM — FPMA President-Elect — and Erica Sed of Quantum Medical Supply explore the critical role compression therapy plays in managing lymphedema and other chronic venous conditions while addressing many of the barriers patients encounter when trying to obtain prescribed treatment.

The conversation highlights the importance of collaboration between healthcare providers, patient education, and access to medically necessary compression therapy. Erica also provides insight into the insurance and documentation process, helping patients and providers better understand how to navigate coverage requirements, reduce delays in care, and improve long-term treatment adherence.

Topics covered

  • The importance of compression therapy in managing lymphedema and chronic venous disease
  • Common barriers patients face in obtaining prescribed compression garments
  • Why patient education and compliance are essential for successful treatment
  • Navigating insurance coverage and documentation requirements
  • Improving patient access through provider collaboration and coordinated care
  • The role of in-home services in supporting adherence and continuity of care
  • Practical ways healthcare providers can help improve patient outcomes through education and access

Whether you're a patient, caregiver, or healthcare professional, this discussion demonstrates how organizations like the Florida Podiatric Medical Association, healthcare providers, and industry partners can work together to improve patient access, strengthen compliance, and ultimately enhance outcomes for individuals living with lymphedema and chronic venous disease.
